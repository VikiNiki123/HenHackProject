Why Create this Site?

This website is all about teaching kids about Media Literacy. As explained before, media literacy is the ability to critically analyze stories present in mass media and being able to determine the creditability and truthfulness of a source. It helps as both user's and consumers to stay informed with the world around us. However, with the Internet, anybody can be a journalist and create articles and state them as facts. This becomes, especially, problematic, when people already assume that people has this skill. Keyword: skill. I find this troublesome for young children. According to recent research, children ages 2 to 8 spend an average of two hours per day, children between 8 and 12 spend four to six hours, and adolescents over 12 years old spend an average of seven to nine hours per day. These kids are growing up with advance technoogy, so people assume that they know how to do alot of things involving it but yet that is not the case. Remember, kids are still native and impressionable, exposing them to the wild west without a good backbone of how to be safe and informed will always end in misguided indualivals that could continue a line of misinformation. But yet, I dont think that only parents and schools should push for this type of education, children need to be able to participate in this form of learning too, whats why this exists; to help children have fun while learning about media literacy.
