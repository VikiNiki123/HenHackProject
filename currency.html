Currency

The Timeliness of Information

In media literacy, currency refers to the relevance and timeliness of information within a media context. It involves assessing how up-to-date and pertinent the content is, ensuring accuracy, authenticity, and diversity of perspectives, while also considering the context in which it's presented. Being currency means being adaptable to emerging trends and technologies, engaging actively with the content, and understanding its implications within society. By developing these skills, media consumers can navigate the media landscape effectively, critically evaluating the information they encounter.

Questions to ask yourself:

  1. When was the information published or posted?
  2. Has the information been revised or updated?
  3. Does your topic require current information, or will older sources work as well?
  4. Are the links functional?
